Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) Loan Sanction Dataset

INTRODUCTION

This project performs a complete Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on the Loan Sanction Training Dataset. The objective is to understand the data structure, clean the dataset, analyze variable distributions, explore relationships between variables, and derive meaningful insights related to loan approval status.

Handling missing values

In[14]:
categorical_cols = ['Gender', 'Married', 'Dependents', 'Self_Employed']

for col in categorical_cols:
    df[col] = df[col].fillna(df[col].mode()[0])

numeric_cols = ['LoanAmount', 'Loan_Amount_Term', 'Credit_History']

for col in numeric_cols:
    df[col] = df[col].fillna(df[col].median())

print(df.isnull().sum())

Insight

	Higher income applicants generally apply for higher loan amounts
	Approved and rejected loans overlap significantly
	Income alone does not determine loan approval

Overall Bivariate Conclusion

	Loan approval is influenced by multiple interacting factors, rather than a single variable.
	Credit history emerges as the most influential bivariate factor affecting loan approval.
	Income and loan amount provide useful context but cannot independently predict loan sanction.
	The observed overlaps across groups highlight the importance of considering multiple features jointly.
